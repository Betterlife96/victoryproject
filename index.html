Is the photovoltaic system worth renting? The NRW consumer advice centre shows what to look out for in solar rental offers. Consumer Advice Centre NRW © Consumer Advice Centre NRW Producing your own solar power, becoming less dependent on energy suppliers and saving money in the long term – that's what the photovoltaic system (PV system) on your own roof stands for.

However, purchase and installation are initially associated with high costs and corresponding planning and administrative effort. So why not just rent the solar system instead of buying it? "There are several larger providers on the market who are active throughout Germany, as well as some regional energy suppliers and municipal utilities that offer PV systems for rent," explains Sören Demandt, energy expert at the NRW consumer advice center. However, such a rental offer can be an alternative to buying if you want to keep the effort and cost risk as low as possible and take into account the comparatively higher total costs." How to compare investments and what to look out for in photovoltaic rental offers has been compiled by the NRW Consumer Advice Centre in the following tips.

Differences between renting and buying a PV system When buying a PV system, a specialist company is commissioned with planning and installation. After commissioning, the owner and operator are fully responsible for the system. This includes all maintenance, insurance and reporting obligations. The advantage lies in full control in the selection of installed components, such as a battery storage system or a wallbox. The disadvantages are a higher investment amount and registration formalities. With a solar system for rent, on the other hand, there is no need for a large initial investment. Instead, a monthly rental fee is paid. The advantage of this is that, in addition to the installation and planning costs, the rental usually also includes additional services such as maintenance and insurance. Over the entire rental period, usually at least 20 years, a fixed monthly rent amount must be paid. The solar system remains in the possession of the provider for the entire duration of the contract.
